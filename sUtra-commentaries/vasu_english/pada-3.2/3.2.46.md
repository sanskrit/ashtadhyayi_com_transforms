---
index: 3.2.46
sutra: संज्ञायां भृतॄवृजिधारिसहितपिदमः

---
Both the words कर्मणि and सुपि of <i>sutra</i> 1 and 4 are understood here and should be applied as the occasion requires. Thus विश्वम्भरा 'earth' (that which supports all); रथन्तर 'the <i>Rathantara</i> <i>Sama</i>'; पतिंवरा 'a girl' lit. (who is about to choose a husband); शत्रुञ्जयः 'an elephant' lit. (who conquers the enemy); युगन्धरः 'a mountain' or 'the pole of a carriage to which the yoke is fixed'; शत्रुंसहः '<i>Satrunsaha</i>' (withstanding an enemy); शत्रुन्तपः '<i>Satruntapa</i>' (destroying an enemy); अरिन्दमः '<i>Arindama</i>' (victorious).



Why do we say 'when denoting a name' ? Observe कुटुम्बभारः 'he who supports his family'.
