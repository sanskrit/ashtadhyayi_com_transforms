---
index: 4.1.172
sutra: कुरुनादिभ्यो ण्यः

---
This debars '<i>an</i>' and '<i>an</i>'. Thus कौरव्यः, नेषव्यः 'the son of <i>Kuru</i> and <i>Nishadha</i>'. The affix denotes 'the king there of' also, as कौरव्यो राजा ॥ How do you explain the form नैषधोर्हति चेदघम् ? This is an archaic or Vedic form. How then in सनैषधस्यार्थपतेः सुतायां ? This is a poetic license.
