---
index: 6.2.50
sutra: तादौ च निति कृत्यतौ

---
Thus प्र꣡कर्ता (with तृन् ) प्र꣡कर्तुम् (with तुमुन्), प्र꣡कृतिः (with क्तिन्) ॥ This <i>sutra</i> debars the <i>Krit</i>-affix accent [[6.2.139]]. Why do we say "before an affix beginning with त्"? Observe प्रज꣡ल्पाकः formed with the affix पाकन् [[3.2.155]], and the <i>Gati</i> प्र, the accent being governed by [[6.2.139]]. Why do we say 'which is नित्'? Observe प्रकर्ता formed by तृच् affix. When a <i>Krit</i>-affix takes the augment इट्, it does not lose its character of beginning with त् on the <i>Vartika</i> कृदुपदेशो वा ताद्यर्थमिडर्थम् ॥ Thus प्र꣡लपिता, प्र꣡लपितुम् ॥ Why do we say "but not before तु"? Observe आगन्तुः꣡ with the <i>Unadi</i> affix तुन् ॥
